# Step 2: Write Email

Use the matched skills and project from Step 1. Pull all personal data from the
Personal Data section. Do not hardcode name, email, phone, or portfolio.

Write the cover email output below.

---

## Email Structure

### Subject Line

- Must contain the exact role title from the job posting
- **MUST include the top 2-3 keywords** from the job posting that you HAVE
  (picked from Keyword Priority in Step 1) — these are the skills mentioned most
  often or emphasized as critical
- **MUST include experience years** if you have 2+ years (e.g. "2 yrs", "3 yrs")
  — this is a filter recruiters scan for
- **NO**: "Applying for [Role]" — weak and obvious
- **YES**: "[Role] — [top keyword], [top keyword], [years]" e.g. "Frontend
  Engineer — Next.js, TypeScript, Tailwind, 2 yrs"
- Under 10 words
- No ALL CAPS, no exclamation marks

---

### Greeting

- Use hiring manager name if available in the posting
- Otherwise: "Hi [Company] Team," or "Hi there,"
- **NOT**: "To Whom It May Concern"

---

### Opening (1–2 sentences) — Pattern Interrupt + Proof

- **Sentence 1 must be a pattern interrupt — not an intro, not a claim.** Open
  with a contrast, a concrete fact, or a surprising detail. Do NOT introduce
  yourself. Do NOT say what you do.
  - ✅ "Most junior developers build todo apps. I built a print-on-demand
    platform real customers use."
  - ✅ "The live URL is urbandtf.app — real users, real orders, real Stripe
    payments."
  - ❌ "Building responsive React applications is what I do."
  - ❌ "I am a frontend developer with experience in React."
- **Sentence 2 (optional):** Anchor with one sharp proof point — a named
  project, a live URL, a specific outcome.

**FORBIDDEN phrases:**

- "I saw your posting" / "I saw your job posting"
- "I am writing to apply"
- "I'd like to apply"
- "I came across your"
- "I am interested in applying"
- "That's what I do" / "Building [X] is what I do"

**Sentence variety rule:** Do not start every sentence with "I". Vary structure
— start with the project name, a URL, a result, or a contrast.

- ❌ "I built... I handled... I also worked on..." — repetitive and
  self-centered
- ✅ "urbandtf.app is live — real customers..." / "The frontend is Next.js with
  TypeScript..." / "Stripe payments process daily..."

Every opening must be unique to this job posting. No template openers.

---

### Proof (2–3 sentences) — Skills + Project Evidence

- **Reference the single MOST relevant project from the Personal Data section by
  name** with its live link — the one that shares the most tech stack overlap
  with this job posting
- Every skill you mention must map to a requirement in the posting — do not list
  skills the job does not ask for
- Mention at most 2 technologies per sentence — always in context of what they
  achieved, never as a list
  - ❌ "I built the frontend with React and TanStack Query, connected to
    Express.js APIs handling Stripe payments and order state"
  - ✅ "The frontend is React with TypeScript, connected to REST APIs for order
    tracking and payments."
- Include at least one measurable or observable result — load time, user count,
  order volume, anything concrete
  - ✅ "Real customers use it to upload designs and check out — it's live at
    urbandtf.app"
  - ❌ "delivering a smooth flow from design upload to checkout"

**FORBIDDEN vague claims:**

- "I also value clear communication"
- "I believe in teamwork"
- "I have experience in modern web applications"
- "I am a passionate developer"

---

### Availability + Close (1 sentence, optional)

- Offer one concrete next step only if it adds value
  - ✅ "Happy to share my backend code from [project]."
  - ❌ "I can share examples if helpful."
- **FORBIDDEN passive offers:** "If you'd like...", "Let me know if..."
- **NO availability filler.** Do not write "available immediately" or "full-time
  remote" — assumed, reads as padding
  - ❌ "I'm available to start immediately and work full-time remotely."
  - ✅ Omit entirely, OR mention only if there's a real constraint (e.g.,
    two-week notice period)

---

### Closing Question (SKIP THIS — 90% of emails should NOT have a question)

**Default: DO NOT include a closing question.** A clean sign-off is better than
a bad question.

Only include one if ALL of these are true:

1. The posting has a genuine ambiguity not answered in the text
2. You are actually curious about the answer
3. A non-technical HR person or recruiter would understand the question

**HR Readability Test:** If an HR person who doesn't code would need to Google a
word, DELETE the question.

---

#### ❌ BAD QUESTIONS — Never Use

- ❌ "Is the frontend work greenfield, or mostly improving an existing
  codebase?" — HR doesn't know what "greenfield" means. Sounds like a vocab
  test.
- ❌ "Are you using Next.js App Router or Pages Router?" — Too technical for a
  first email.
- ❌ "What's your CI/CD pipeline look like?" — No one asks this in a cold email.
- ❌ "Does your roadmap lean toward Next.js?" — Sounds like a tactic, not real
  curiosity.
- ❌ "Let me know if you're interested" / "Looking forward to hearing from you"
  — Not a question, just passive filler.
- ❌ Any question whose answer is already visible in the posting.

#### ✅ Good Question — Rare

- ✅ "Is this role focused on new product builds or maintaining existing
  features?" — Simple, any HR person gets it.
- ✅ "Will this person work solo or with a frontend team?" — Clear and
  practical.

---

**When in doubt: delete the question.** No question is infinitely better than a
confusing one.

---

### Sign-off (all from Personal Data — do not hardcode)

- Full name (Section 1 — Profile)
- Portfolio URL (Section 1 — Profile)
- Phone number (Section 1 — Profile)
- Social links (Section 1 — Social Profiles) — only include if posting
  explicitly asks for them

Format: `[Name] · [Portfolio URL] · [Phone]`

---

## Final Validation Checklist

Run this before writing the final email:

- [ ] Subject line: role title present, top 2-3 keywords included, years
      included (if 2+), under 10 words, no "Applying for"
- [ ] Opening is a pattern interrupt — NOT a self-intro or "Building X is what I
      do"
- [ ] Zero forbidden phrases from any rule above
- [ ] No sentence stacks more than 2 technologies
- [ ] At least one measurable or observable result is present
- [ ] Every skill maps to a posting requirement
- [ ] One project named from projects.md with live link
- [ ] No availability filler ("available immediately", "full-time remote")
- [ ] Closing question OMITTED (preferred) OR simple enough for a non-technical
      HR person to understand — no jargon like "greenfield", "CI/CD", "App
      Router"
- [ ] No passive endings
- [ ] Under 180 words (body only)
- [ ] No buzzwords
- [ ] No skill gaps disclosed — lead with what you have
- [ ] **Readability test:** Read the email aloud. If any sentence sounds like a
      tactic from a playbook — rewrite it. Must sound like a competent human,
      not someone following instructions.
