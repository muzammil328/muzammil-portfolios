# Step 1: Extract & Match

Read the job posting above and the Personal Data section (do not modify it).

No files are written in this step. All analysis stays in working memory and
passes to Step 2 (email) and Step 3 (suggestion).

---

## Extract from Job Posting

- **Company** (name if mentioned, otherwise "the company")
- **Target role** (most specific match)
- **Experience level** (junior, mid, senior)
- **Required skills** (list explicitly mentioned — e.g. HTML, CSS, JavaScript,
  React, Node.js, TypeScript, Next.js)
- **Nice-to-have skills**
- **Tech stack keywords**
- **Location/remote requirement**
- **Industry/domain** (if stated)
- **Pain signal** — What problem are they hiring to solve? Name it in one
  sentence.
- **Tone** — Formal / startup / technical — determines email voice
- **Mirror words** — 3–4 exact phrases from the posting to reflect back in the
  email
- **Keyword priority** — Which 2-3 skills are mentioned MOST often or emphasized
  as critical (e.g. repeated, bolded, listed first)?

---

## Match Against Personal Data

For each required skill from the job posting, check the Personal Data section:

| Job Requirement                                      | Match                    | Project Evidence                  |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| [skill] (e.g. HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Node.js) | HAVE / PARTIAL / MISSING | [project name from Personal Data] |
| ...                                                  | ...                      | ...                               |

For each nice-to-have: mark HAVE / MISSING.

**Cross-reference ALL sections of Personal Data, plus the CV:**

- Section 2 (Skills) — for technology skills
- Section 5 (Projects) — for project-based proof
- Section 4 (Experience) — for professional experience
- Section 3 (Education) — for degree requirements
- CV — for anything not already captured in the sections above (real facts
  only, never invented)

---

## Calculate Fit Score

- **Strong** = 80%+ must-haves covered
- **Moderate** = 50–79% covered
- **Weak** = below 50% → consider skipping

---

## Identify for Email (passed to Step 2)

- **Best matching project** — most relevant to this role
- **Top skills to lead with** — required skills you HAVE, with project proof
- **Secondary value skills** — from your profile that add context

---

## Missing Skills

- **Missing skills** — required skills you have zero evidence for (list these in
  the final output)

---

## Where You're Weak (visible only in this step, never in the email)

Before writing the email in Step 2, call out plainly:

- Every MISSING or PARTIAL requirement from the Match table, with why it's
  weak (no evidence in Personal Data or CV, or only tangential evidence)
- Whether the Fit Score is Strong / Moderate / Weak and what's driving that
  score
- One honest sentence on whether this role is worth applying to given the
  gaps found

This section is diagnostic, for the user's eyes — Step 2's email must never
disclose gaps or apologize for missing skills; it leads with what's HAVE
only.
